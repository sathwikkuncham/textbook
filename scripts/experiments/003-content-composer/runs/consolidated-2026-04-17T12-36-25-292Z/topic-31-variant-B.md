### 1. The Dominant Locus: PSORS1 and the HLA-Cw6 Allele

Psoriasis is a polygenic disease, meaning it requires the interplay of multiple genes. However, one specific region completely dominates the genetic landscape, accounting for 35% to 50% of the disease's heritability. 

Before we name the specific genes, let's think about what psoriasis fundamentally is: an autoimmune-driven inflammatory response. Therefore, it makes sense that the major genetic determinant lies squarely in the Major Histocompatibility Complex (MHC) region, which controls how our bodies present antigens to T-cells. 

This major locus is called **PSORS1**, located on chromosome **6p21.3**. 

Within the PSORS1 locus, the single most powerful genetic determinant for psoriasis is the **HLA-Cw6** allele (specifically, the sequence variant HLA-C\*06:02). 

**But why is HLA-Cw6 so critical?**
Examiners love it when you connect a genetic label to actual pathophysiology. HLA-C is an MHC Class I molecule. The job of MHC Class I is to present intracellular peptides to **CD8+ T cells**. In a patient with the HLA-Cw6 allele, the binding pocket of this MHC molecule is perfectly shaped to erroneously present certain self-antigens (autoantigens). 

The two highest-yield autoantigens to mention in your exam are:
1.  **LL-37 (Cathelicidin):** An antimicrobial peptide released by damaged skin cells.
2.  **ADAMTSL5:** A protein produced by melanocytes.

When HLA-Cw6 presents these autoantigens to CD8+ T cells in the epidermis, it triggers the initial pathogenic immune cascade.

### 2. Clinical-Pathological Correlation of HLA-Cw6

In dermatology, genetics directly dictate the clinical phenotype. Highlighting this correlation will set your answer apart from an undergraduate's.

A patient who is HLA-Cw6 positive typically presents with **Type I Psoriasis**. You can summarize the clinical hallmarks of this genotype for your exam as follows:
*   **Early Onset:** Usually presenting before age 40 (peak onset in late teens to twenties).
*   **Strong Family History:** High penetrance across generations.
*   **Specific Morphologies:** Strong association with guttate psoriasis (often triggered by streptococcal throat infections) and a high severity of plaque psoriasis.
*   **Koebner Phenomenon:** A high likelihood of developing lesions at sites of physical trauma.

### 3. The Polygenic Landscape: PSORS2 through PSORS9

While PSORS1 provides the "antigen-presenting match" that sparks the disease, the rest of the genetic landscape dictates how aggressively the skin reacts. These are the non-PSORS1 loci, numbered PSORS2 through PSORS9. 

You do not need to write a thesis on all of them, but listing them with their specific functional disruptions demonstrates comprehensive PG-level knowledge. To make them easy to memorize, group the high-yield ones by their **function**:

**Group A: Immune Amplification (The Inflammatory Gas Pedal)**
*   **PSORS2 (Chromosome 17q25):** This locus contains the *CARD14* gene. CARD14 is an epidermal scaffold protein that strongly activates NF-κB, a master transcription factor for inflammation. Mutations here mean the keratinocytes are hyper-reactive to any immune signal. *(Note: CARD14 mutations are also heavily implicated in generalized pustular psoriasis).*

**Group B: Epidermal Barrier Defects (The Leaky Shield)**
*   **PSORS4 (Chromosome 1q21):** This region contains the Epidermal Differentiation Complex (EDC). Specifically, deletions in the Late Cornified Envelope genes (*LCE3B* and *LCE3C*) are found here. Without these proteins, the skin's physical barrier is compromised, making it easier for environmental triggers (like trauma or microbes) to initiate inflammation.

**Group C: The Supporting Loci (Briefly list for completeness in a 10-marker)**
To round out the polygenic picture, you should list the remaining loci. You don't need deep mechanistic explanations for these unless specifically asked, but including their chromosomal locations shows mastery:
*   **PSORS3:** Chromosome 4q34 
*   **PSORS5:** Chromosome 3q21 
*   **PSORS6:** Chromosome 19p13 
*   **PSORS7:** Chromosome 1p 
*   **PSORS8:** Chromosome 16q 
*   **PSORS9:** Chromosome 4q31
